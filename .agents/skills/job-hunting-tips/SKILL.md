# Job Hunting Tips

## When to use this skill

- User wants job search strategies
- User needs interview preparation tips
- User asks about salary negotiation
- User wants general career advice
- User needs motivation/guidance during job search

## Job Search Strategy

### Where to Look
- LinkedIn Jobs (set alerts for your stack)
- RemoteOK, WeWorkRemotely (remote positions)
- Indeed, Glassdoor (general)
- AngelList (startups)
- Company career pages (direct apply)
- Twitter/X, Reddit r/cscareerquestions (opportunities)

### Application Strategy
- Quality > Quantity: 10 tailored apps > 50 generic
- Apply within 24 hours of posting (first 48 hours matter)
- Track all applications in spreadsheet
- Follow up after 5-7 business days if no response

### Priority Order
1. Referrals (highest success rate)
2. Direct to hiring manager
3. Company career page
4. Job boards

## Interview Tips

### Before Interview
- Research company: mission, product, recent news
- Study job description, match keywords to your experience
- Prepare 3-5 stories using STAR method
- Prepare 3-5 questions for interviewer
- Test tech setup (if remote)
- Have water nearby

### Common Questions
- "Tell me about yourself" - 2 min pitch
- "Why this company?" - Show research
- "Strengths/Weaknesses" - Be honest, show growth
- "Tell me about a challenge" - STAR story
- "Why should we hire you?" - 3 key points

### Technical Interviews
- Practice coding: LeetCode (Easy/Medium)
- Explain your thinking out loud
- Start with brute force, optimize later
- Ask clarifying questions
- Test your solution

### Questions to Ask
- "What's the biggest challenge the team faces?"
- "How do you measure success in this role?"
- "What's the team culture like?"
- "What's the growth path for this position?"

## Salary Negotiation

### Research
- Use levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Payscale
- Know your value: experience + skills + location
- Have a number in mind before they ask

### Negotiation Tips
- Never give the first number
- Negotiate base + equity + benefits
- Get everything in writing
- Don't accept on the spot (sleep on it)
- Have a walk-away number

### Template Response
```
"Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the role. 
Based on my research and experience, I was expecting 
[X]. Is there flexibility on the compensation?"
```

## General Tips

### CV/Resume
- Keep it to 1-2 pages max
- ATS-friendly: no graphics, simple formatting
- Tailor for each application
- Key skills in first 1/3 of document

### LinkedIn
- Professional photo
- Compelling headline (not just "Software Developer")
- Complete all sections
- Get recommendations
- Post regularly

### Networking
- Connect with recruiters at target companies
- Attend meetups, conferences
- Informational interviews
- Help others - karma matters

### Stay Productive
- Set daily/weekly goals
- Track metrics: applications, responses, interviews
- Learn something new daily
- Take breaks - avoid burnout

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Applying to everything = desperation
- Not customizing CV = auto-reject
- No follow-up = missed opportunities
- Badmouthing past employers = red flag
- Not preparing questions = disinterest
- Accepting first offer = leaving money on table

## Timeline Expectations

| Stage | Typical Time |
|-------|--------------|
| Application → Response | 1-2 weeks |
| Interview → Offer | 2-4 weeks |
| Total process | 1-3 months |

Stay persistent - the right role will come.
